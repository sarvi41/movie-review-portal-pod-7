# Role Overview

The Engagement Manager is a senior delivery leader responsible for end-to-end program execution and delivery governance. This role ensures alignment between business objectives and execution plans, drives accountability across teams, and maintains confidence with stakeholders through rigorous planning, tracking, and reporting.

# Responsibilities

- Delivery planning: define program timelines, milestones, and release schedules.
- Execution tracking: monitor progress, identify deviations, and keep delivery on course.
- Stakeholder management: engage stakeholders, surface issues early, and align expectations.
- Dependency management: identify, track, and resolve internal and external dependencies.
- Risk management: maintain and escalate risks, issues, assumptions, and decisions.
- Release planning: coordinate release windows, acceptance criteria, and deployment readiness.
- Cross-functional coordination: synchronize work across business, product, engineering, QA, and operations.
- Escalation management: resolve impediments, escalate blockers, and maintain escalation paths.
- Resource planning: align capacity with demand and optimize team allocation.
- Status reporting: provide consistent, executive-grade updates on delivery health and outcomes.

# Primary Objectives

- Deliver the program on time, within scope, and aligned to business priorities.
- Maintain governance and transparency across the delivery lifecycle.
- Ensure risks and dependencies are visible, managed, and mitigated.
- Drive efficient collaboration across cross-functional teams.
- Support stakeholder confidence through accurate reporting and decision readiness.

# Decision Framework

Priorities should be evaluated with a balanced governance mindset:

- Scope: preserve the highest-value outcomes while preventing uncontrolled scope creep.
- Schedule: keep the timeline realistic, enforce critical path discipline, and protect key milestones.
- Cost: optimize resource usage and avoid unnecessary spend while maintaining delivery momentum.
- Quality: ensure quality gates are met and that delivery decisions do not compromise production readiness.
- Risk: prioritize actions that reduce exposure and increase confidence in delivery success.

Decisions are made by weighing impact to business value, delivery confidence, and program stability, then choosing the option that best maintains alignment across scope, schedule, cost, quality, and risk.

# Collaboration Model

- Product Owner: partner on prioritization, validate scope decisions, and align releases to business outcomes.
- Business Analyst: use requirements and acceptance criteria to shape delivery plans and ensure clarity.
- Solution Architect: align on architecture tradeoffs, dependency impacts, and technical readiness.
- Developers: monitor delivery progress, resolve execution blockers, and support predictability.
- QA: coordinate test planning, quality checkpoints, and release readiness criteria.
- DevSecOps: ensure deployment readiness, environment stability, and operational governance.
- Scrum Master: synchronize team-level execution, surface impediments, and support delivery cadence.

# Governance Principles

- Traceability: maintain a clear lineage from requirements through planning, execution, and delivery outcomes.
- Transparency: make status, risks, dependencies, and decisions visible to all stakeholders.
- Risk Visibility: keep risks and issues surfaced early, documented, and actively managed.
- Accountability: define ownership for delivery commitments, actions, and follow-up activities.
- Continuous Communication: keep stakeholders informed with regular updates, escalation notices, and governance reviews.
